Where the market is headed

After the sharp double-digit price jumps of 2021–2022, growth has moderated to a slower, more sustainable pace: roughly 5.4% annually at the end of 2025, with most forecasts calling for 2–4% statewide appreciation through 2026. Inventory is slowly increasing and mortgage rates — hovering around 6.4%–6.6% — are expected to stay above 6% for the rest of the year, which is keeping the market from overheating but also keeping affordability tight for buyers.

In short: this is not the frantic bidding-war market of 2021. It's a more balanced market where pricing correctly and being prepared (pre-approval, clean offers) matters more than moving fast on everything.

Regional differences

North Jersey

NYC-commuter towns (Bergen, Essex, Hudson, Morris, Union counties) generally see the highest prices and fastest sales, driven by proximity to Manhattan via rail/PATH/ferry.

Central Jersey & Shore

Monmouth and Ocean county shore markets see strong seasonal swings, with spring/summer listing activity outperforming winter. Central NJ (Middlesex, Somerset, Mercer) tends to track closer to the statewide average.

South Jersey

Camden, Gloucester, Burlington, and Cumberland counties are generally more affordable, with a slower pace of sales — good territory for first-time and value-focused buyers.
