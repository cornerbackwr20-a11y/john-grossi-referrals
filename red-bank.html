Why a Red Bank-specific agent match matters

Red Bank packs a remarkable range into just 1.75 square miles. A walkable, year-round downtown, Navesink riverfront homes, condos, and a fast train to Manhattan mean buyers here run the full spectrum — first-time condo buyers, luxury riverfront, and commercial investors alike — and pricing swings hard depending on which corner of the borough you're in. Whether you're after a downtown condo, a riverfront home, or a commercial space, the agent I match you with will actually specialize in that lane.

Local notes for Red Bank buyers and sellers

Downtown, riverfront & everything between

Prices span from condos around $300K to riverfront homes past $1.2M. The northern neighborhoods and Navesink waterfront sit at the top; western sections are more affordable. In a market this varied, truly comparable sales are everything.

A walkable, year-round town

Broad Street's 60-plus restaurants, independent boutiques, and the Count Basie Center for the Arts keep Red Bank active all year, not just in summer. That steady demand is a real draw for buyers and a genuine selling point for sellers.

Commute & competition

NJ Transit's North Jersey Coast Line reaches Manhattan in roughly 65 minutes, and limited inventory in a small borough keeps things competitive. Pre-approval and a sharp local agent are worth a lot here.

Red Bank's median sale price has run in the low-$600s in mid-2026, but with condos, family homes, and riverfront estates all in the mix, the headline number means little without a local read. See the Market Info page or ask me for current comps.
